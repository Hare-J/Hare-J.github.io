Who am I?

My name is James and I am a full-stack software developer in Welland and graduate of Niagara College's Computer Programmer Analyst program.

I have a wide variety of experience in all facets of software development, as both an individual and a team member, building everything from SQLS data warehouses to complex point of sales systems for a local food charity in Niagara Falls. Some of the projects I'm most proud of, or even just had the most fun working on, can be found in the Work section of this site or on my GitHub page at Hare-J.

I am extremely passionate about building quality software following solid object-oriented design for ease of future use and development.

Experience

Niagara College Peer Tutoring

During my second and third terms I was hired by the college as a peer tutor for a number of classes and helped over a dozen students to understand difficult concepts in C#, Javascript, and Python.

Surprisingly, to me at least, this was immensely helpful for my own learning as well, as it forced me to research simpler topics that might have been skimmed over otherwise. Additionally, it gave me extra practice programming simple apps through creating example and practice work for the students I tutored to help give more context to the content they were learning in class.

Community Project

During my fourth term with the college I helped work on a custom point-of-sales system for a local food charity.

For this project I was part of a team of six students with the goal of designing and developing this system from the ground up, working with the client from the beginning to deliver the features they wanted to streamline different day-to-day tasks for their business. We ended up creating this system as a web app using C# and SQLite with the Model-View-Controller design pattern and spent four months total working on it.

My Mission Statement

Every piece of work you create represents you as a person and should be completed to the highest possible (and realistic) standard.

While it can often be tempting to take the easy way through things, I have found that doing so rarely leads me to a place of positive growth.

A team is only as strong as its ability to support its weakest members.

In the past there have been many situations where I have been the one offering support, but also many where I felt completely alone and lost, and these have been my absolute worst experiences both for project outcome and growth. For the good of everyone, and for the good of the project, it is always the right thing in my experience to support your team above all else.

Failure is an important contributor to growth and should be treated as a valuable learning experience.

Some of the most important and lasting experiences during my development as a professional and as a person have been failures. Obviously it's better to avoid failure when at all possible, but that isn't always an option and if you're going to fail you might as well accept the discomfort and take away as much as you possibly can from it.
